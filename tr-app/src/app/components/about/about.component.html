Running Tacoma since 2010

The Tacoma Runners began in January 2010 with a few words on Twitter. Three people confirmed for an easy run from Tacoma's iconic Parkway Tavern. Seven showed up. Twelve runners came for week two from the Red Hot. Then sixteen the following week. After more than a decade, several thousand runners have joined us and the group continues to grow. All events are open to runners of all speeds and abilities. We won't lose you.

We have two weekly runs - a Thursday night social run and a free Saturday 5k. These are a great way to meet people and learn about all the various running options in the South Sound.

Our Board

Our board is made of volunteer runners who are dedicated to ensuring there is a free, fun, and inclusive running community in the South Sound. Feel free to ask us any questions or just say hi!

Deb McLaren - Vice President and Saturday Run Host

Favorite Run - Point Defiance of course!

First Run was Saturday, January 12, 2013

My first Saturday run with Tacoma Runners 11 years ago was the beginning of a life change for me. I had recently turned 50 and had never run a 5k. I was so intimidated! I was sure I would be last or get lost or both! After finishing that first run, I could barely walk and thought I would never run again. That day, Kent and I decided that running every Saturday was non-negotiable. Each week it got easier and gave me the confidence to join a Thursday run, do several half-marathons, and even a few Sprint triathlons. We (along with John) began hosting the Saturday run seven years ago when Derek and Sarah had Owen. Tacoma Runners has always promoted itself as a "social group that happens to run." I have met and logged in over 500 runners who have joined us on Saturday (at least once). I joke that I see my Tacoma Runner friends more regularly than my family! Tacoma Runners has a place for every age and level of runner. Come and find your place with us.

John Hellman - Board Member

Joined 2012

I started my running life back in 1968 when after running a timed mile in P.E. I couldn't go to school the next day.
Cross-Country and Track were the logical next step. I pursued these through Junior High and High School. During summers I'd get together with my teammates and train endlessly (for fun!) in the California heat.
What followed was Cross-Country at USC, San Jose State, and finally at U.C. Santa Cruz.
Running in the Redwoods and pastureland (chased by crazed, red-eyed cows) gave me the speed to run my PR 2:36 marathon at The Avenue of the Giants in 1977.
At UCSC (Home of the Fighting Banana Slugs) I coached the Men's and Women's Cross-Country Teams. Fun and improvement were our guiding philosophy. After some further education at the University of Oregon and some Middle School and High School coaching jobs, Peace Corps and then marriage, my wife Cassie and I headed north to Seattle in 1990.
I've been running with the Tacoma Runners since 2012.
Join Course Marshal Mochi and the gang every Saturday at Point Defiance for the Tacoma Runners FREE 5K!

Mike Bogatay - Board Member

My brother had sold Tacoma Runners as a fun run with a beer at the end, but all I could think about was the burning in my legs and lungs. His words of encouragement were met by expletive laden requests for his silence as I agonized over each step of a long and gradual incline. A little over ten years ago I was a burnt-out workaholic smoker hobbling my way to the end of a Thursday run. I survived, enjoyed my beer, and connected with a cool cross-section of Tacoma's community. The friendships I found fueled a lifestyle change to countless 5k's, many relays, and even a good number of half, full, and ultramarathons. Running changed my life, and if it hasn't already, it can change yours too.
